Why SABR?

The Black model assumes volatility is constant across strikes — but real markets show a volatility smile. Out-of-the-money options trade at different implied vols than ATM options. SABR was built specifically to:

  • Capture the empirically observed smile and skew in interest rate markets
  • Provide an analytically tractable approximation (Hagan formula)
  • Allow traders to interpolate and extrapolate the vol surface consistently
  • Maintain arbitrage-free dynamics (under certain parameter constraints)
  • Give intuitive parameters that map to observable market features

The Four Parameters

  • α (Alpha) — The initial level of stochastic volatility. Directly controls the ATM implied vol level. Think of it as "where is the smile centered vertically?"
  • β (Beta) — The CEV exponent determining the backbone. β=0 gives a normal model (additive), β=1 gives lognormal (Black). Often pre-set by convention.
  • ρ (Rho) — Correlation between rate and vol shocks. Negative ρ creates a skew (lower strikes have higher vol). Controls asymmetry of the smile.
  • ν (Nu) — Vol-of-vol. Controls the curvature of the smile. Higher ν makes the wings steeper — out-of-the-money options become relatively more expensive.

SABR in Practice

In interest rate derivatives desks, SABR is the workhorse model for:

  • Swaption vol surfaces — calibrated per expiry/tenor pair
  • Cap/floor smiles — per caplet expiry
  • CMS pricing — convexity adjustments require the full smile
  • Risk management — smile delta and vega via parameter sensitivities
  • Exotic calibration — base layer for more complex models (LMM-SABR)

Key Insight

SABR's power lies in its parsimonious parametrization. With just four numbers (α, β, ρ, ν), you can capture the entire shape of the volatility smile for a given expiry. This makes it ideal for:

Quoting conventions — Instead of quoting vol at every strike, traders communicate SABR parameters. A complete smile is transmitted in four numbers.

Dynamic hedging — The model predicts how the smile moves when rates move (the "backbone" dynamics), enabling more accurate delta hedging.
